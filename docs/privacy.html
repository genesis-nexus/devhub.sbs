Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 26, 2025

At DevHub ("we," "our," or "us"), we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website https://devhub.sbs (the "Site").

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using the Site, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please do not use the Site.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide to Us

We collect information that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Subscribe to our newsletter
  • Contact us through our contact forms or social media
  • Participate in surveys or promotions
  • Leave comments on our blog posts

This information may include:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Any other information you choose to provide

1.2 Information Automatically Collected

When you visit our Site, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior, including:

  • Device Information: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type
  • Usage Information: Pages visited, time spent on pages, links clicked, referring website
  • Location Information: General geographic location based on IP address
  • Cookies and Similar Technologies: Information collected through cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies

1.3 Tool Usage Data

Important Privacy Feature: Most of our developer tools (Base64 encoder/decoder, URL encoder/decoder, JSON validator, JWT decoder, Hash generator, etc.) process data entirely in your browser using JavaScript. This means:

  • Your data never leaves your device
  • We do not store, log, or transmit the content you encode, decode, or validate
  • All processing happens client-side for maximum privacy

For certain features that require server-side processing (such as the OIDC playground), we may temporarily process data but do not store it permanently.

3. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

3.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us provide and improve our services. We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our Site and store certain information.

3.2 Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as:

  • Theme preferences (light/dark mode)
  • Session management
  • Security features

Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our Site. These cookies collect information such as:

  • Number of visitors
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Traffic sources
  • User demographics and interests

Google Analytics data is anonymized and aggregated. For more information, visit Google's Privacy Policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

Advertising Cookies (Google AdSense)

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements on our Site. Google AdSense uses cookies to:

  • Display relevant ads based on your interests
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Measure ad effectiveness

For more information about Google's advertising practices and to opt-out, visit https://www.google.com/policies/technologies/ads/.

3.3 Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in various ways:

  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or delete existing cookies
  • Opt-Out Tools: Use tools like Google's Ad Settings (https://adssettings.google.com) to manage ad preferences
  • Do Not Track: We respect Do Not Track signals when enabled in your browser

Note: Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of certain features on our Site.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • HTTPS Encryption: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using SSL/TLS
  • Client-Side Processing: Most tools process data locally in your browser without transmission
  • Access Controls: Limited access to personal information by authorized personnel only
  • Regular Security Audits: Periodic review of security practices and vulnerabilities
  • Secure Hosting: Our website is hosted on secure infrastructure with regular updates

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.
